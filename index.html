Basic Brain Anatomy

The basics are important.

Image courtesy Depositphoto.

Major Parts of the Brain

There are 3 main parts to the brain: the cerebrum, the cerebellum, and the brain stem.

Lobes of the Brain

There are 4 lobes to the brain: the frontal lobe, the occipital lobe, the parietal lobe, and the temporal lobe.



Select Key Systems

A bit more info on key brain systems...

Image courtesy Depositphoto.


Prefrontal Cortex

The prefrontal cortex is one of the most important parts of the brain. It’s responsible for our higher thoughts and reasoning. Like the name implies, it sits at the front of the brain.

Limbic System

The limbic system is made up of several complex structures. It is largely responsible for one’s emotions, memory, and regulation of the body’s autonomic functions. It’s seated in the middle of the brain, just above the brain stem.

Cerebellum

The cerebellum sits at the base of the brain at the rear of the skull. It is responsible largely for balance, coordination, and involuntary bodily functions.



Picturing the Brain

Several ways technology has enabled us to view the brain...

Image courtesy Depositphoto.

MRIs

MRI stands for magnetic resonance imaging. It is a procedure used to provide highly detailed diagnostic imagery of one’s brain.

Head CTs

CT scans, also known as CAT scans, are called head CTs when done on the head to visualize the brain. They provide a less-detailed, but faster result than MRIs do. They are often used during emergencies to diagnose head trauma.

Connectomes

Unlike the other two items listed, connectomes are not a diagnostic tool. They are, however, a fascinating way to present the connections in the brain, providing a glimpse into its complexity. Connectomes are often compared to wiring diagrams.



Interesting Facts

A few interesting facts people might like to know...

Image courtesy Depositphoto.

Number of Neurons

The human brain contains, on average, 86 billion neurons.

Whole Hog

The brain weighs an average of 3 pounds and are largely made of fat. However, they represent almost a quarter of the body’s daily energy consumption. This is why thinking can actually make you feel tired.

Brain Consistency

Unlike many organs in the body, the brain does not naturally hold its shape if removed. Many people have actually described the texture and consistency of the brain as similar to oatmeal.
